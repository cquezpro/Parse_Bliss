Start by identifying the single problem that is troubling you most. It could be a source of stress, discomfort, anger or sadness


Example: No matter how hard I work my boss is critical and unsatisfied.


How is this problem an opportunity for growth? How could dealing with it make you a better person? What new skills could it help you develop? Are there tangible benefits that may come from dealing with this problem effectively?

Example: Dealing with a critical boss is an opportunity to learn human psychology. If I can learn to impress someone this demanding, then I'll be able to easily satisfy customers when I start my own business. While she is sometimes unreasonable, her demands also give me the opportunity to get even better at what I do.


How do you choose to respond to the situation?

What values and strengths will you use in your response?

Example: I'm good at reading people and can use that to anticipate things she'll likely object to in my work and prepare a response. I know my own value and will not let her affect my opinion of myself. If she becomes unreasonable I will smile to myself and think of her as an irate customer complaining to world-class customer service rep. This will be great experience for the future - I'll use her as an example of how to deal with angry people when I'm training my employees at my new company.
